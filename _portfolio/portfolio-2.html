--- title: "Protein-protein interactions within cellulosome of Clostridium thermocellum" excerpt: "Investigating the differential interaction preferences of enzyme-bearing dockerins for cohesin domains using biophysical characterization." collection: portfolio order: 4 duration: "Dec 2023 – Apr 2025" status: completed header: teaser: project-cellulosome.jpg --- Cellulosomes are multi-enzyme complexes highly efficient at degrading cellulose. This research explores the intricate network of protein-protein interactions that govern how these complexes self-assemble. ### Key Approaches: * **Affinity Chromatography:** Purifying recombinant proteins using Ni-NTA and size-exclusion chromatography. * **Binding Kinetics:** Quantifying the thermodynamic binding affinities between diverse enzymes bearing dockerin and cohesin domains using **Microscale Thermophoresis (MST)** and Size Excluusion Chromatography. * **Computational Analysis:** Utilizing various tools to study, model and map interactions to hypothesise interaction preferences. *Note: This work has resulted in a co-authored structural biology manuscript, currently under review — [read the pre-print here](https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.01.19.700288).*
