### Abstract
Cellulosomes are highly efficient, multi-enzyme complexes responsible for breaking down crystalline cellulose. The precise spatial arrangement and assembly of these complexes rely heavily on high-affinity, specific protein-protein interactions between dockerin domains on the enzymes and cohesin domains on the scaffolding protein. This study explores the structural and thermodynamic preferences driving these differential interactions, clarifying how *Clostridium thermocellum* self-assembles its diverse enzymatic machinery. 

### Key Contributions
* Quantified binding kinetics and thermodynamics of diverse dockerin-cohesin pairs using **Microscale Thermophoresis (MST)** and spectroscopy.
* Optimized high-yield purification protocols for multi-domain recombinant cellulosomal proteins using affinity and size-exclusion chromatography.
* Developed data analysis pipelines to map interaction network matrices.
